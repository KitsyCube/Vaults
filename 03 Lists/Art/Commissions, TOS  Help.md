Paypal - DO NOT state anywhere on the invoice, in notes, or anywhere on the platform that it's for NSFW commissions or even for artwork. Send the finished piece out via a different email that isn't tied to your PayPal account. If you start making crazy money per month you'll want to move to a different processor anyway, but small scale they don't care.

Details of the timeline in the Terms & Conditions box:
- What you're delivering (medium, size, file format if digital)
- Turn around time or deadline. If they haven't provided a deadline, give a turnaround time that leaves you plenty of extra
- What stages you'll provide updates at - eg thumbnail sketch, initial outline, inking, coloring, whatever is relevant to your style
- How many revisions they're allowed, and what the extra fee is for each additional change

**Essential Components Checklist**:
- **Header:** Clearly state "Invoice".
- **Artist Info:** Name, Address, Email, Phone.
- **Client Info:** Name and Contact Details.
- **Items:** Detailed description of the commission, including size, medium, and number of characters.
- **Costs:** Itemized prices, taxes, and total.
- **Dates:** Invoice date and payment due date.
- **Terms:** Payment methods and usage rights.

- **Include Vital Details:** Clearly list the project name, description of services, revisions allowed, total price, and any applicable taxes or shipping.
- **Establish Payment Terms:** Specify if you require a 50% non-refundable deposit before starting and the final payment before delivering the high-resolution file.
- **Assign Unique Invoice Numbers:** Use a system (e.g., 2026-001) to track, organize, and manage your income for tax purposes.
- **Send and Follow Up:** Send the invoice via email after the client agrees to the terms, providing a direct link for payment (e.g., PayPal button).
